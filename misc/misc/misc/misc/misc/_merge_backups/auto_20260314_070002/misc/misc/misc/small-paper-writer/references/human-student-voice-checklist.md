# Human Student Voice Checklist

Use this as a final pass before delivering a draft. The goal is not to make the paper sloppy. The goal is to make it sound like a real high-school student team that actually did the work.

## 1. Motivation check
- Does the paper explain why this topic came from a real trigger?
- Is the trigger specific, such as class exposure, campus observation, family need, or a news case?
- Did the draft avoid spending too long on giant background statements?
- Can the reader tell why this student team chose this topic, not just why the topic exists?

Good signs:
- mentions a real scene
- mentions how the topic was noticed
- explains why the issue felt worth testing

## 2. Purpose check
- Are the research purposes written as clear, bounded tasks?
- Does each purpose match a method that actually appears later?
- Are the verbs realistic: 了解, 比較, 分析, 設計, 實作, 驗證可行性?
- Did the paper avoid oversized purposes that the method cannot support?

Warning sign:
- purpose sounds bigger than the data, time, or prototype can justify

## 3. Literature review check
- Does each literature paragraph help the paper make a later decision?
- After introducing a concept, does the draft explain why it matters for this study?
- Is the literature organized by theme, category, or comparison instead of source-by-source copying?
- Is there at least one sentence that bridges literature to method?

Good bridge patterns:
- 因此，本研究選擇______。
- 綜合上述文獻可知______，故本研究將聚焦於______。

## 4. Method realism check
- Are the tools, platforms, versions, devices, or datasets named clearly?
- Does the draft mention counts, ratios, scope, or workflow steps?
- Would a reader roughly understand how the work was done?
- Does the method section sound like someone actually built, collected, tested, or compared something?

Strong authenticity signals:
- exact software/tool names
- data amount
- test environment
- failed or limited conditions
- step order

## 5. Results check
- Do the results first describe what was observed before making a claim?
- Are figure/table references used naturally when relevant?
- Are claims moderate rather than dramatic?
- Does the text stay close to the actual result instead of jumping to world-scale implications?

Prefer:
- 由圖／表可知______
- 根據結果顯示______
- 在本次測試下，______
- 與原先預期相比，______

Avoid:
- 證明了重大影響
- 完全解決問題
- 顯示革命性成果

## 6. Limitation check
- Does the paper admit at least 1-3 concrete limitations?
- Are the limitations specific rather than symbolic?
- Does the conclusion explain what future work would realistically improve?

Common realistic limitations:
- sample size too small
- collection time short
- one-school or one-class scope only
- lighting/background interference
- hardware or free-tool restrictions
- unstable model performance in complex scenes

## 7. Word-choice check
- Did the draft replace vague praise words with concrete nouns or situations?
- After every abstract benefit word, is there a specific explanation?
- Did the paper avoid stacking polished empty transitions?

High-risk words that need grounding:
- 提升效率
- 優化體驗
- 增加便利性
- 重要價值
- 深遠影響
- 多元發展

If one of these appears, ask:
- for whom?
- in which step?
- compared with what?
- under what condition?

## 8. Voice check
- Does the tone stay formal but not professor-like?
- Is there some student perspective left in the draft?
- Can the paper naturally use 我們 / 本組 / 本研究 where appropriate?
- Does the writing sound like a team reporting what they learned and built?

Good signs:
- modest confidence
- learning-through-doing tone
- honest reflection after testing
- not trying to sound omniscient

## 9. Rhythm check
- Do paragraphs vary in length slightly?
- Are some transitions simple and direct instead of over-designed?
- Does the draft avoid making every paragraph equally polished and balanced?

Human-feeling rhythm often includes:
- one denser technical paragraph
- one shorter observation paragraph
- one direct limitation sentence

## 10. Final anti-AI pass
Ask these fast questions:
- If I remove the topic nouns, does this still sound like a real student experience?
- Does the paper contain details only the actual writer would know?
- Is the literature helping the study instead of just filling space?
- Are the conclusions narrower than or equal to the evidence?
- Does the paper sound like a finished school research project instead of a general article?

## Quick scoring pass
Mark each item Yes / Needs work.
- real motivation trigger
- realistic, bounded purposes
- literature-to-method bridge
- concrete method detail
- result-first reporting
- explicit limitations
- grounded wording
- student-researcher voice
- varied rhythm
- no inflated ending

If 3 or more items are "Needs work," rewrite before delivery.
